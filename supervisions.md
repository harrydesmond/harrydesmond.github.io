I have had the privilege of supervising a wonderful group of postdocs, PhD students and undergraduates over the years. Below I list my postdoctoral and doctoral supervisees, with a summary of what we have worked on together.
{: style="text-align: justify"}

### Postdoctoral researchers

**Indranil Banik** (Portsmouth, 2024 --) [[our papers](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/search/q=author%3A%22Desmond%2C+H%22+author%3A%22Banik%2C+I%22&sort=date+desc)]
{: style="margin-bottom: 0"}
Local solutions to the Hubble tension and the (in)homogeneity of the local Universe.
{: style="text-align: justify; margin-top: 0.2em"}

**Ricardo Landim** (Portsmouth, 2022 -- 2024) [[our papers](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/search/q=author%3A%22Desmond%2C+H%22+author%3A%22Landim%2C+R%22&sort=date+desc)]
{: style="margin-bottom: 0"}
Constraining screened scalar-tensor theories of gravity using galaxy dynamics.
{: style="text-align: justify; margin-top: 0.2em"}

### PhD students

**[Deaglan J. Bartlett](https://deaglanbartlett.github.io/)** (Oxford DPhil, 2019 -- 2022) [[our papers](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/search/q=author%3A%22Desmond%2C+H%22+author%3A%22Bartlett%2C+D%22&sort=date+desc)]
{: style="margin-bottom: 0"}
Thesis *"Fundamental physics from galaxies"*: field-level tests of modified gravity and dark matter. Also symbolic regression. Now a postdoctoral fellow at Oxford.
{: style="text-align: justify; margin-top: 0.2em"}

**[Tariq Yasin](https://www.physics.ox.ac.uk/our-people/yasin)** (Oxford DPhil, 2019 -- 2023) [[our papers](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/search/q=author%3A%22Desmond%2C+H%22+author%3A%22Yasin%2C+T%22&sort=date+desc)]
{: style="margin-bottom: 0"}
Thesis *"Combining kinematic and photometric constraints on the galaxy--halo connection"*: Bayesian modelling of HI line profiles and comparison to empirical models of the galaxy-halo connection. Also tests of cosmic anisotropy with clusters. Now a postdoctoral fellow at Oxford.
{: style="text-align: justify; margin-top: 0.2em"}

**[Richard Stiskalek](https://richard-sti.github.io/)** (Oxford DPhil, 2022 -- 2026) [[our papers](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/search/q=author%3A%22Desmond%2C+H%22+author%3A%22Stiskalek%2C+R%22&sort=date+desc)]
{: style="margin-bottom: 0"}
Thesis *"Constrained simulations of the local Universe as a laboratory for precision cosmology and astrophysics"*: Leveraging constrained large-scale structure simulations for new tests of cosmology and galaxy formation: the cosmography of the local Universe, peculiar-velocities, the distance ladder, H&#x2080; inference and various other things.
{: style="text-align: justify; margin-top: 0.2em"}

**[Alicia Martin](https://www.physics.ox.ac.uk/our-people/martina)** (Oxford DPhil, 2023 --) [[our papers](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/search/q=author%3A%22Desmond%2C+H%22+author%3A%22Martin%2C+A%22&sort=date+desc)]
{: style="margin-bottom: 0"}
Machine-learning fundamental physics from cosmology, with a focus on symbolic regression of halo properties.
{: style="text-align: justify; margin-top: 0.2em"}

**Antonio Najera** (Portsmouth, 2024 --) [[our papers](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/search/q=author%3A%22Desmond%2C+H%22+author%3A%22Najera%2C+J+A%22&sort=date+desc)]
{: style="margin-bottom: 0"}
New handles on the cosmic distance ladder: investigating and developing alternative distance-calibration methods, and models for the Hubble tension.
{: style="text-align: justify; margin-top: 0.2em"}
