I am a final-year DPhil student in astrophysics at the University of Oxford, working with [Harry Desmond](https://www.port.ac.uk/about-us/structure-and-governance/our-people/our-staff/harry-desmond), [Julien Devriendt](https://www.physics.ox.ac.uk/our-people/devriendt), and [Adrianne Slyz](https://www.physics.ox.ac.uk/our-people/slyz). My research focuses on the dynamics of the nearby Universe, using constrained cosmological simulations, Bayesian inference, and machine learning to link observations with theoretical models. I am particularly interested in the galaxy–halo connection, large-scale cosmic flows, and the interface between cosmology and gravitational-wave astronomy.

Most recently, I held a [Pre-Doctoral Fellowship](https://www.simonsfoundation.org/flatiron-institute-center-for-computational-astrophysics-pre-doctoral-program/) at the Flatiron Institute, working with [Shy Genel](https://users.flatironinstitute.org/~sgenel/) and [Lucia A. Perez](https://luciaperez.owlstown.net) on the application of cosmological rescaling to dark matter merger trees. In 2024, I was based at the Institut d’Astrophysique de Paris, where I worked with [Guilhem Lavaux](https://www.guilhem-lavaux.eu) on Bayesian field-level reconstructions of the local Universe. As part of my M.Sc. in Physics at the Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich, I collaborated with [Miguel Zumalacárregui](https://miguelzuma.github.io) and [Marius Oancea](https://physik.univie.ac.at/en/about-us/staff/staff-detail/user/oanceam21/inum/1163/backpid/48532/) on strong-field gravitational lensing of gravitational waves. During my undergraduate degree at the University of Glasgow, I worked with [John Veitch](https://www.veitch.me.uk) and [Chris Messenger](https://www.gla.ac.uk/schools/physics/staff/christophermessenger/) on gravitational-wave data analysis.
